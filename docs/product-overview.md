# Nature Audio Tour — Product Overview

## What It Is

A QR-code-based audio guide that lets park visitors learn about exhibits — rare plants, stone structures, water bodies, and landmarks — by scanning physical markers placed throughout the park. No app install required — scan, tap, listen.

## How It Works

1. Visitor sees a small aluminum marker with a QR code near an exhibit
2. They scan with their phone camera → opens a mobile webpage
3. Page shows an exhibit photo, language selector, and "Listen" button
4. They tap listen → 60-90 second audio plays about the exhibit (origin, facts, significance)
5. Ads are displayed on the page (splash, banner, post-audio)

## Who It's For

- **Visitors**: Get a richer park experience without staring at their phone
- **Park (Experium)**: Premium feature that differentiates them, plus passive revenue share
- **Sponsors**: Physical brand placement on markers + digital ad impressions from a hyper-local audience
- **Us**: Platform revenue from direct-sold ads

## First Deployment

**Experium Park**, Hyderabad — 150-acre premium park with rare plant species, stone structures, lakes, and landmarks. 500+ daily visitors at ₹1000/entry.

Target: ~50 QR markers covering rare/interesting exhibits across the park.

## Core Principles

- **Zero friction**: No install, no mandatory login, one tap to content
- **Audio-first**: Screen is content-light; the value is in listening while observing the exhibit
- **Scans are the product**: Business model is Model B — page impressions drive revenue, audio is the hook that justifies QR codes being there
- **Indirection**: QR codes encode a permanent short ID; content is mutable server-side
- **Markers are dumb, server is smart**: No exhibit info printed on markers — allows reassignment without reprinting
